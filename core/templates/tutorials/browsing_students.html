{% extends 'tutorial_base.html' %} {% block tutorial_title %} Browsing Students {% endblock %} {% block instructions %}

Step 1: From your homepage, begin by searching by keyword or clicking "Browse Students".

There is also a "Browse Students" link in the left side navigation menu.

Step 2: Click "Show Details" to view a student's details or click "Show All Details" to see everyone's.

Step 3: Filter students by a variety of attributes grouped into four different categories.

Use this to find students who satisfy a particular requirement, such as speaking a language or being a graduating senior.

Step 4: Search students' resumes by keyword/skills, or search for individuals by first/last name.

Results will by default be ordered by relevance. However, you can also modify in what order and how many results appear.

Step 5: Star and unstar students for easy later referral by clicking on the star icon below their name.

Alternatively, star and unstar multiple students at once by selecting the checkboxes on the left of their name and selecting a dropdown option under "More Actions". Any student that you star will also appear starred to other recruiters at your firm. The student will never know that you starred him/her.

Step 6: To view starred students, select the "Starred Students" student under "Choose Student List".

Step 7: Leave comments on students' profiles for your and your coworkers' later referral.

Anything that you write will only be visible to you and other recruiters at your firm. The student will never know what you wrote.

Step 8: Learn about campus orgs and majors by clicking on the ones that are links.

The number of campus organizations about which we have info will go up with time.

Step 9: See which students have attended your events in the past by noting the icon by their name.

Hovering over the icon will show you which events that student attended and on what date.

Step 10: Modify the order in which and how many results appear.

  • If you filter by an attribute which a student has not yet filled out, that student will not appear in the results. e.g. If you filter for students with an SAT of 2000 or above, any student who hasn't specified his/her SAT will not appear in the results.
  • If you have a free trial subscription, then you will only be able to search and browse your events' RSVPs and attendees.

  • Results will by default be ordered by relevance. For search, this means relevance towards your query. For filtering, it means that the most recently updated profiles will appear at the top.
