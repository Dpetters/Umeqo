{% extends 'tutorial_base.html' %} {% block tutorial_title %} {% block tutorial_title_tab %} Cancelling Upcoming Events & Deadlines {% endblock %} {% endblock %} {% block instructions %}

Step 1: From your homepage, navigate to the upcoming event or deadline that you wish to cancel.

All of the upcoming events are under the "Upcoming" header in the "Your Events and Deadlines" block.

Step 2: Choose a name, type, and a privacy level for your event or deadline.

Private events are only visible to students whom you send invitations to. Public events and deadlines are visible to everybody.

Step 3: Decide whether you want students to be able to drop off their resumes without RSVP-ing.

If the resume drop is enabled, there will be an additional list of students for you to browse through called "Event Name Resume Drop".

Step 4: Add a map to your event by typing the location and clicking on a matching suggestion.

If you chose a type of deadline as the event type, then a location is not require and the field will be entirely hidden.

If none of the suggestions match the location, simply don't select any of them. The only difference is that your event page will not include a map.

Step 5: Fill in the description for your event or deadline.

There is no need to mention the intended audience of the event in the description. That can be done in step 8.

Step 6: Look over the existing events when picking a time and date to avoid possible conflicts.

Ideally your event will not fall in the same timespan as another event that might attract a similar audience.

Step 7: Select the date and time of your choice.

Note that the event can't start in the past.

Step 8: Pick the intended audience for your event by selecting one or more school years.

Step 9: If there is important info that everyone who RSVPs should know, put it in the RSVP field.

When a student RSVPs, the information you include in this field will be displayed to them in the form of a dialog.

Step 10: Create the event or deadline by clicking the button at the bottom.

The students subscribed to your firm for updates will receive an email notifying them of this new event or deadline.
